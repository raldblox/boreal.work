# Boreal Deck Guide

This document keeps Boreal's pitch deck, demo video, launch video, marketing copy, and knowledge-base structure aligned to one product story.

## 1. Core Positioning

### Canonical product line

**Submit one request. Boreal finds the best way to fulfill it.**

### Expanded version

Boreal helps people complete work without choosing tools first.  A request starts in chat, Boreal checks executable supply first, then routes to the right provider, product, agent, or human team and keeps delivery, payment, and proof in one thread.

### Internal language stack

- **Interface layer:** chat-native
- **Category layer:** request-native commerce
- **System thesis:** intent-to-fulfillment infrastructure

### User-facing terms

- **Request**: something a person wants done, found, bought, or delivered
- **Offer**: something a person, merchant, tool, or agent publishes to be hired or bought
- **Proposal**: a scoped response to a request
- **Work thread**: the conversation, approvals, delivery, and proof attached to one request
- **Checkout**: the direct purchase path when an offer is already buyable

### What not to say

- Do not force users to think in terms of `callable providers`, `supply registries`, or `tool routing`.
- Do not describe humans as only a fallback.  Some work should go to humans or hybrid teams first.
- Do not overclaim escrow, generalized on-chain settlement, or full ACP/UCP execution if the flow is not live.

## 2. Product Narrative

### The simple story

1. A user has a job, need, or problem.
2. They submit one request in chat.
3. Boreal decides the best fulfillment path.
4. If direct supply exists, the work can resolve fast.
5. If it does not, Boreal opens the request to the right market.
6. The full trail stays attached: matching, proposal, approval, delivery, payment, review.

### The two-sided thesis

- **Instant path:** when supply already exists, Boreal resolves it fast.
- **Market path:** when supply does not already exist, Boreal opens the right market around the request.

This contrast should anchor every public artifact.

## 4. Demo Video Guide

### Goal

Show one complete request-to-outcome loop.  The video should feel like a product in motion, not a slide deck.

### Recommended 60-second structure

1. User submits a request.
2. Boreal structures it.
3. Matching engine shows why the path was chosen.
4. Instant path:
   direct offer, provider, or workflow is selected.
5. Delivery and evidence attach back to the same thread.
6. Contrast beat:
   a harder request opens to the market instead of pretending automation can solve it.

### Demo thesis

**When supply already exists, Boreal resolves it fast.  When it does not, Boreal opens the right market around the request.**

### Demo rules

- No scene numbers.
- No presenter notes on screen.
- No feature-tour copy overload.
- Keep the UI real.
- Show why something matched, not just that it matched.
- End on accountability: delivery, proof, request closure.

## 5. Launch Video Guide

Launch video is broader than the product demo.  It can introduce the company and market.

### Launch video should do four things

1. Define the problem.
2. Show the product in one real flow.
3. Explain why this is a new market layer.
4. Leave viewers with one memorable idea.

### Best closing idea

`One request in. Best fulfillment path out.`

## 6. Marketing Guide

### Homepage

The homepage should lead with:

- one clear promise
- one clear prompt-first action
- a visible path into the market

### Good homepage framing

- one request
- no tool switching
- provider, product, agent, or human route
- tracked work thread

### Avoid

- abstract infra language in the hero
- over-explaining protocols before the product promise lands
- calling everything `AI` when the stronger framing is routing and fulfillment

### Messaging ladder

- **Hero:** one request, no tool switching
- **Secondary:** Boreal chooses the best fulfillment path
- **Proof:** direct supply, proposals, delivery, payment, review
- **Category:** request-native commerce

- accurate about what is live now
