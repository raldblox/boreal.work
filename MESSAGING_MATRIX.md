# Boreal Messaging Matrix

This document turns Boreal's positioning into audience-specific messaging.

Use it for:

- landing pages
- decks
- outbound messages
- partner docs
- investor updates
- product copy

## Master Message

Boreal is building request-native commerce: an intent-to-fulfillment layer for work, products, and services.

Short public version:

Boreal is a chat-native market for request-native commerce.

## Shared Rules

Across every audience:

- start with the problem of disappearing demand
- explain Boreal through requests, supply, proposals, fulfillment, and checkout
- keep the alpha boundary explicit
- use `chat-native` for interface
- use `request-native` for category
- use `intent-to-fulfillment` for thesis

## Audience: Buyers

### What they care about

- getting something done
- finding the right supplier quickly
- not losing context
- comparing options clearly
- seeing progress and delivery in one place

### Lead line

Post what you need in plain language. Boreal turns it into a request, checks existing supply first, and opens proposals when the work is custom.

### Supporting points

- start in chat
- match supply first
- compare proposals with price, timing, and deliverables
- keep fulfillment attached to the request

### Good CTA

- `Post a request`
- `Open Boreal`

## Audience: Sellers / Supply-side operators

### What they care about

- discoverability
- better inbound demand
- clear request context
- easier selling and response workflows
- being reachable by humans and agents

### Lead line

List your service, product, or agent capability once, then let Boreal match it to relevant requests and buying intent.

### Supporting points

- public supply profile
- request-side demand visibility
- proposal workflows for custom work
- digital listings and supported checkout flows

### Good CTA

- `List your supply`
- `Browse Supply`

## Audience: Agents / developers building agent flows

### What they care about

- structured demand
- routable supply
- interoperability over time
- machine-readable state
- execution paths beyond chat answers

### Lead line

Boreal turns plain-language demand into structured requests that can be matched to supply, proposals, fulfillment workflows, and supported checkout paths.

### Supporting points

- durable request object
- supply normalization
- routing architecture
- provider-backed execution foundation
- deeper protocol support as roadmap work

### Good CTA

- `Explore the product surface`
- `Read the whitepaper`

## Audience: Partners / marketplaces / service providers

### What they care about

- distribution
- integration surface
- demand flow
- normalized ingestion
- attributable routed traffic

### Lead line

Boreal is a request-native commerce layer that can route demand into partner supply instead of leaving that demand trapped in chat or search.

### Supporting points

- service-provider normalization
- external supply sync
- provider-backed invocation
- future protocol depth

### Good CTA

- `Review integration direction`
- `Discuss supply ingestion`

## Audience: Investors

### What they care about

- category definition
- why now
- wedge
- expansion path
- defensibility

### Lead line

Boreal sits between work marketplaces and agentic commerce: a chat-native interface for request-native commerce, with a longer-term thesis around intent-to-fulfillment infrastructure.

### Supporting points

- demand is already being expressed in chat, prompts, and agent flows
- current products either stop at answers or split work and commerce into different systems
- Boreal unifies requests, supply, proposals, fulfillment, and checkout
- the alpha is already a usable market surface
- protocol depth and settlement expand later from that base

### Good CTA

- `Read the whitepaper`
- `See what is live today`

## Audience: Press / broad public

### What they care about

- a simple definition
- why it matters
- what is new

### Lead line

Boreal is a chat-native market where people and agents start with a request, then move through supply, proposals, fulfillment, and checkout in one place.

### Supporting points

- work, products, and services in one market
- starts with plain-language demand
- keeps outcomes visible
- public alpha

## Message Ladder

### 5-word level

`Commerce starts with a request.`

### 10-word level

`Boreal turns chat into requests, then routes them to fulfillment.`

### 20-word level

`Boreal is a chat-native market for request-native commerce, connecting requests to supply, proposals, fulfillment, and supported checkout.`

### 50-word level

`Boreal is building request-native commerce: an intent-to-fulfillment layer for work, products, and services. People or agents start with a plain-language request. Boreal turns that into a durable workspace, checks existing supply first, opens proposals when the work is custom, and keeps delivery or checkout visible until the outcome is complete.`

## Terms By Audience

### Safe for almost everyone

- request
- supply
- proposal
- fulfillment
- checkout
- public alpha

### Mostly for technical and investor audiences

- request-native commerce
- intent-to-fulfillment
- provider-backed execution
- protocol interoperability

### Use carefully or avoid in first-touch messaging

- orchestration platform
- agent registry
- protocol-native settlement
- intent-to-task
- AI gatekeeper

## Practical Rule

If the message does not explain both:

- how Boreal starts
- what Boreal keeps attached until the outcome is done

then it is probably too vague.
