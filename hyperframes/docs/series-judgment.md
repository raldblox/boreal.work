# Boreal Video Series Judgment

This note records how the later three-video brief was adapted so it matches the actual Boreal app and roadmap.

## Theme And Font Judgment

The brief's mood is useful.  Its literal theme and font stack are not a match for `next-app/`.

### What does match

- dark, calm, infrastructural tone
- no stock-photo human footage
- no neon gradients or decorative effects
- deliberate transitions and low-hype pacing

### What does not match

- the shipped app is light-first, not pine-dark as the primary surface
- the shipped app uses `Syne`, `Manrope`, and `Geist Mono`
- the shipped app does not use `Playfair Display` or `DM Mono`

## Product Claim Judgment

Some of the brief's proof points overstate what the repo can claim today.

### Safe to show

- chat-native request creation
- structured request workspaces
- public supply directory
- ranked matches with score breakdowns
- proposal and fulfillment workflows
- provider-backed checkout paths
- Privy-backed x402 initiation for supported routes
- activity and audit trails

### Not safe to show as live

- Solana escrow locked on acceptance
- automatic released settlement as a live product default
- full `BM25 + vector + LLM rerank` as shipped end to end
- exact quarter promises that the roadmap does not explicitly commit to
- `free to start` as a guaranteed product claim from repo evidence alone

## Adaptation Rules For This Series

1. Keep the brief's confidence and structure.
2. Switch product-proof scenes to Boreal's actual light UI language.
3. Use dark graphite wrappers only for hook and close moments.
4. Replace unsupported escrow language with payment-aware and audit-aware language.
5. Replace unsupported matching-stack claims with persisted match candidates, score breakdowns, lexical plus embedding foundation, and structured filters.
6. Use `public alpha` and `live today` instead of stronger commercialization claims the repo does not prove.
