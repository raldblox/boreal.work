# Boreal Architecture 150 Design

## Style Prompt

This cut should feel like a moving systems diagram, not a product ad and not a generic startup animation.  It needs to explain how Boreal works end to end: where requests enter, how supply is normalized, how matching branches, how work or checkout stays attached to the same request, and where the feedback loop compounds.  Use dark graphite wrapper scenes for the thesis and the architecture close, but let most proof scenes live on Boreal's real light-first surfaces with cyan route traces and calm operational cards.

## Typography

- `Syne` for section statements and architectural thesis lines
- `Manrope` for explanatory copy and node descriptions
- `Geist Mono`, `IBM Plex Mono`, `monospace` for routes, tiers, object labels, and machine-state tags

## Colors

- `#071311` deep graphite
- `#F7FAF9` near-white surface
- `#EAF4F2` soft panel fill
- `#22C1F3` Boreal cyan
- `#62FFCE` mint confirmation

## Rules

- diagram-first, not stock-UI-first
- distinguish `alpha today` from `target architecture`
- no literal claims of shipped escrow or automatic on-chain settlement
- no fake dark-mode product recreation for live UI layers
- motion should reveal system structure, not decorate it
