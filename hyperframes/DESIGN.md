# Boreal HyperFrames Design

## Style Prompt

Boreal should feel like a bright operational market surface under pressure: cold, directional, and precise without drifting into black-box AI aesthetics.  Scenes should read as request routing infrastructure, not a generic startup promo.  Use light work surfaces, graphite framing, cyan-first signals, and a restrained northern glow that supports the product rather than overwhelming it.

## Mood

- technical
- editorial
- calm under load
- directional
- credible

## Canvas

Light-first scenes with deep graphite transitions and closers.  Dark scenes are allowed for the problem statement and final category close, but the system should still read Boreal-cyan-first rather than purple-first.

## Colors

- `#F7FAF9`: near-white work surface
- `#071311`: deep graphite structure
- `#22C1F3`: Boreal cyan signal
- `#62FFCE`: mint route highlight
- `#6537C6`: restrained logo-depth accent

## Typography

- `Space Grotesk`: headlines, section statements, product-defining claims
- `IBM Plex Mono`: rails, chips, proof labels, request metadata

## Motion

- primary transition: medium-energy push slide with a light blur handoff
- entrance language: staggered panels, route-line sweeps, vertical lifts, measured scale-ins
- easing palette: `power3.out`, `expo.out`, `sine.inOut`, `back.out(1.2)`

## What Not To Do

- do not make the cut purple-led
- do not default to black-neon AI trailer visuals
- do not invent marketplace nouns that replace `Supply` or `Requests`
- do not use bubbly consumer motion or playful illustration systems
- do not turn Boreal into a generic dashboard ad disconnected from the product thesis

## Practical Notes

- Boreal's product uses `Syne` and `Manrope`, but this video workspace intentionally swaps to `Space Grotesk` plus `IBM Plex Mono` to stay sharp on video without falling into the overused AI-demo look.
- Keep numbers tabular and labels sparse.
- Decorative gradients should stay localized, not full-screen and noisy.
