# Boreal full GTM plan

Last updated: 2026-04-27

## 1. Executive summary

Boreal should go to market as the simplest truthful promise in the product:

`Submit one request. Boreal finds the best way to fulfill it.`

The GTM strategy should not lead with protocol depth, AI abstraction, or feature abundance. It should lead with a buyer problem that is already painful and easy to recognize:

- people know the outcome they want
- they do not know which tools, agents, providers, or freelancers to assemble
- chat gives answers, but not always accountable completion
- marketplaces show listings, but do not start from the request

Boreal wins when it is understood as:

- one request in
- best fulfillment path out
- the full trail stays attached: matching, proposal, delivery, checkout, and proof

The recommended launch motion is:

1. win one narrow paid wedge first
2. use that wedge to collect testimonials, proof, and workflow data
3. expand into the broader request-native market story
4. layer in developer and ecosystem distribution after the buyer story is already legible
5. expand into the agent-owner story once the wedge and buyer workflow are easy to understand

## 2. What Boreal is selling right now

### Canonical public line

`Submit one request. Boreal finds the best way to fulfill it.`

### Expanded explanation

Boreal is a chat-native market for request-native commerce. A user starts with the outcome they need. Boreal turns that into a durable request, checks direct executable supply first, opens proposals when the work is custom, and keeps delivery or checkout attached to the same work thread.

### Product truth boundary

Market only what is clearly supported today:

- request workspace
- public requests and offers
- proposals and fulfillment workflows
- direct offers and buyable listings
- provider-backed execution for supported paths
- supplier-side inbox flows
- machine-readable request-first surfaces

Do not lead with claims about:

- universal escrow
- complete protocol-native settlement
- total autonomy across all work categories
- generalized mainnet-grade commerce infrastructure

## 3. GTM objective

### Primary objective for the next 60 to 90 days

Prove that real buyers will pay Boreal to take one scoped request, route it through the best available path, and return a usable outcome without making the buyer assemble the stack.

### Secondary objectives

- establish Boreal's language in the market
- generate first case studies and testimonials
- identify which request categories convert fastest
- validate whether the wedge can scale with acceptable human-assist rates
- create reusable content from real request flows

## 4. Market thesis

### Core market insight

Demand is increasingly expressed in prompts, chat, and agent workflows, but fulfillment is still fragmented across tools, APIs, providers, marketplaces, and human operators.

### Why now

- users are already starting from chat
- AI increases the volume of expressed demand
- buyers want outcomes, not tool assembly
- supply is fragmented across humans, products, services, and agents
- programmable payment and agent ecosystems are becoming real enough to matter

### Boreal category position

Use three layers of language:

- interface layer: `chat-native`
- category layer: `request-native commerce`
- thesis layer: `intent-to-fulfillment infrastructure`

Public marketing should spend most of its time at the interface and category layers.

## 5. Ideal customer profiles and sequencing

## ICP 1: early-stage founders shipping in the next 30 days

### Why start here

- urgent pain
- high willingness to pay for speed
- easy to reach through public channels and direct outreach
- output quality is easy to judge
- outcomes are easy to package into a fixed offer

### Jobs to be done

- write launch copy fast
- prepare listing copy
- shape onboarding copy
- ship without assembling prompts, tools, and freelancers

### Pain points

- too many tools and tabs
- not enough time
- weak or generic copy from one-shot prompts
- no single place to track delivery and revision

### Boreal promise

`Submit one launch request. Get launch-ready copy without choosing tools first.`

## ICP 2: indie builders and operators with repeated small execution needs

### Why next

- adjacent to founder wedge
- recurring demand
- likely to value reusable request workflows
- good fit for fixed-scope offers and direct paths

### Example use cases

- landing page copy refreshes
- product descriptions
- offer listing cleanups
- short marketing asset generation
- research-plus-draft requests

## ICP 3: agent owners, agent developers, and technical operators

### Why later in the GTM stack

- strategically important
- useful for ecosystem growth and integrations
- not the best lead audience for simple commercial messaging
- strongest after Boreal is already legible as a buyer workflow

### Best role in GTM

- validate programmable surfaces
- share technical credibility
- drive registry, API, and agent ecosystem adoption
- introduce Boreal as a work network for agents, not just a chat surface

### Message

`Give your agent a work network. Install Boreal so your agent can find jobs, post requests, track progress, deliver outputs, and get paid through one shared work surface.`

## ICP 4: suppliers and service operators

### Why important

Demand-side proof should lead, but supplier growth must begin early enough to ensure quality routing.

### Message

`Publish your offer once. Let Boreal route clearer demand to you through requests, proposals, and direct checkout paths.`

## 6. Beachhead wedge

### Recommended wedge

Launch Copy Pack

### Offer shape

- buyer: founders launching in the next 30 days
- fixed price: use the repo's current wedge pricing as the initial test baseline
- SLA: 24 hours
- deliverables:
  - headline and subheadline
  - 3 to 5 benefit sections
  - onboarding copy block
  - checkout-ready listing draft
  - one revision pass

### Why this wedge is strong

- sharp, legible problem
- easy landing page promise
- clear before/after transformation
- direct demonstration of request routing
- bounded scope
- easy to operationalize manually at first

### What this wedge validates

- willingness to pay
- quality of request intake
- routing quality
- human-assist rate
- speed to delivery
- acceptance and revision behavior
- testimonial potential

## 13. Sales and conversion playbook

### Initial sales motion
High-touch, founder-led, and wedge-focused.

### Conversion rule
Do not convert vague demand with vague offers. Keep scope tight enough that the buyer understands exactly what they get.

### Objection handling

#### `Why not just use ChatGPT?`
Because the problem is not only generating words. The problem is turning one request into accountable delivery with the right path, revision flow, and proof attached.

#### `Why not hire a freelancer directly?`
Because Boreal checks the fastest executable path first and only opens custom work when the request actually needs judgment.

#### `What if my request is broader than the pack?`
Then Boreal should reroute or reject it instead of pretending a narrow wedge covers everything.

## 17. Risks and mitigations

### Risk: messaging gets too abstract
Mitigation: lead every asset with a buyer problem and one visible workflow.

### Risk: Boreal sounds too broad to trust
Mitigation: sell one narrow wedge first and keep boundaries explicit.

### Risk: too much protocol language confuses buyers
Mitigation: keep protocol and ecosystem depth in developer surfaces, not in first-touch sales copy.

### Risk: human assist becomes invisible agency work
Mitigation: set tight scope rules and reject requests that exceed the wedge.

### Risk: supplier-side quality is uneven
Mitigation: start with a controlled fulfillment path before opening the market too wide.
